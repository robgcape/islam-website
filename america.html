Islam in America

A rich history shaped by resilience, diversity, and community.

History of Islam in the U.S.

Islam has been present in America since the 1600s, brought by enslaved West African Muslims. Despite oppression, many preserved elements of their faith and culture.

Today, Islam is one of the fastest-growing religions in the United States, represented by African-American, Arab, South Asian, West African, Latino, and many other communities.

Key Themes

Early History

Enslaved African Muslims formed the earliest Muslim presence in the Americas.

Modern Growth

Immigration and conversion have expanded the Muslim population nationwide.

Community Impact

Muslims contribute to education, healthcare, business, politics, and social justice.
