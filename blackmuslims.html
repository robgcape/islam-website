African-American Muslims

A legacy of resilience, leadership, and spiritual transformation.

History & Influence

African-American Muslims have played a defining role in shaping Islam in the United States. From the early presence of enslaved West African Muslims to the rise of influential leaders in the 20th century, their contributions continue to inspire communities nationwide.

Key Figures & Movements

Nation of Islam

A major movement founded in 1930 that emphasized Black empowerment and self-determination.

Malcolm X

A transformative civil rights leader whose journey through Islam reshaped American history.

Modern Influence

Today, African-American Muslims continue to lead in activism, scholarship, and community building.
