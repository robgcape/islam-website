The Holy Qur'an

The divine revelation that guides the lives of over a billion Muslims worldwide.

What Is the Qur'an?

The Qur'an is the sacred scripture of Islam, believed by Muslims to be the literal word of God revealed to the Prophet Muhammad over a period of 23 years. It is written in Arabic and is considered the final revelation in a long line of scriptures that includes the Torah and the Gospel.

Muslims recite, memorize, study, and reflect on the Qur'an as a source of spiritual guidance, moral clarity, and personal transformation. Its verses address topics such as justice, compassion, worship, family life, community, and the nature of God.

How the Qur'an Was Revealed

According to Islamic tradition, the Qur'an was revealed to the Prophet Muhammad through the Angel Gabriel beginning in the year 610 CE. These revelations continued throughout Muhammad’s life, addressing both spiritual teachings and real-life situations faced by the early Muslim community.

The revelations were memorized by the Prophet’s companions and written down on parchment, leather, bone, and other materials. After the Prophet’s passing, the Qur'an was compiled into a single written text to preserve it for future generations.

Structure of the Qur'an

The Qur'an is divided into 114 chapters, known as Surahs, which vary in length from a few lines to several pages. Each Surah is made up of verses called Ayat.

The Qur'an is not arranged chronologically. Instead, chapters are generally ordered from longest to shortest, with the exception of the opening chapter, Al-Fatiha, which is recited in every Muslim prayer.

The Qur'an in Daily Muslim Life

The Qur'an plays a central role in Muslim worship and daily life. Muslims recite verses in their five daily prayers, memorize chapters for spiritual reflection, and study its teachings to guide their personal and moral decisions.

Many Muslims strive to memorize the entire Qur'an, earning the title of Hafiz. Qur'anic recitation is considered an art form, with rules of pronunciation known as Tajweed that preserve the beauty and clarity of the original Arabic.

Key Concepts

Revelation

The Qur'an was revealed through the Angel Gabriel, beginning in the year 610 CE.

Structure

It contains 114 chapters (Surahs) and is written in classical Arabic.

Role in Daily Life

Muslims recite, memorize, and reflect on the Qur'an as a source of spiritual guidance.
